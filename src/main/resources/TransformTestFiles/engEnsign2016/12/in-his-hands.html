“In His Hands,” Ensign, December 2016

In His Hands

By Heather Kyle

The author lives in Utah, USA

Clay,

limp,

shapeless,

yet with potential.

The Master’s

kind heart,

loving eyes

see what I can become.

Slowly He works,

sculpting,

molding

patiently, so painfully.

At first

I resist—

I cannot see

why or how.

He persists.

He shapes and molds

firmly,

yet somehow gently.

A shapeless lump of

clay

becomes

a masterpiece.
